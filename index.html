Self-Stabilizing Spoon

Empowering People with Parkinson’s Disease

What is it?

The Self-Stabilizing Spoon is a smart assistive device designed to help people suffering from Parkinson’s disease eat more comfortably. It uses motion sensors and motors to detect hand tremors and stabilize the spoon in real-time.

Why is it Needed?

Parkinson’s disease often causes hand tremors that make it difficult to eat independently. This can lead to frustration, loss of dignity, and dependence on others. A self-stabilizing spoon improves quality of life and restores confidence during meals.

Key Features

  • Built-in gyroscope and accelerometer
  • Real-time tremor compensation using motors
  • Rechargeable battery with long life
  • Easy to clean and food-safe design
  • Interchangeable spoon and fork heads

How Does It Work?

The spoon uses sensors to detect unintended hand movements. A microcontroller processes the signals and activates small motors to counteract the tremors, keeping the spoon head steady while the user eats.

Benefits

  • Increases user independence
  • Improves mental well-being
  • Reduces mess and frustration
  • Supports caregivers by easing mealtime difficulties
